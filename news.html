News & Guides - FAQ

How often is the casino blacklist updated?

Our casino blacklist is reviewed and updated every month. We monitor active player complaints filed with licensing authorities (MGA, UKGC, Curaçao), forum reports, and our own withdrawal tests. If a casino resolves all flagged issues, it may be removed from the list.

How do I check if an online casino is legit?

Check for a valid gambling licence (displayed in the footer - usually MGA, UKGC or Curaçao). Verify the licence number directly on the regulator's website. Look for an eCOGRA or iTech Labs seal, read recent player reviews, and confirm withdrawals are processed within the stated timeframe. Our review process covers all of these for every casino we feature.

What are the fastest withdrawal casinos in 2026?

Crypto-based no-KYC casinos consistently offer the fastest withdrawals — often under 10 minutes for Bitcoin and USDT. Among fiat casinos, those using e-wallet payouts (Skrill, Neteller) typically process within 24 hours. See our full withdrawal speed comparison in the guides section above.

What is the difference between RTP and house edge?

RTP (Return to Player) and house edge are two ways of expressing the same thing. A slot with 96% RTP has a 4% house edge - meaning the casino retains 4% of all wagers over time, and the player receives 96% back. Higher RTP = lower house edge = better odds for the player.

What should I do if my casino won't let me withdraw?

First, complete all KYC verification documents - most withdrawal delays stem from unverified accounts. If verification is complete and withdrawals are still delayed beyond the stated window, contact support in writing and keep records. If unresolved after 48 hours, file a formal complaint with the casino's licensing authority. For MGA-licensed casinos, use the official MGA complaint portal.
